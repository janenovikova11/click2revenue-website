# How to Get More Solar Quotes on the Sunshine Coast

The Sunshine Coast is one of the highest solar penetration regions in Australia. Clean Energy Regulator 2024 data shows residential solar adoption across the SC is consistently above national averages - and with power bills continuing to climb, the demand for new installations and battery system upgrades is not slowing down.

The problem for most SC solar businesses is not a lack of demand. It is a lead generation system that bleeds budget on the wrong channels, generates low-intent form submissions, and has no automated follow-up to convert enquiries into booked quotes before a competitor does.

Getting more solar quotes on the Sunshine Coast is a three-part challenge: get in front of the right homeowners, respond before the other businesses do, and follow up consistently enough to convert the comparison shoppers who take two to four weeks to decide.

---

## Why Your Current Solar Ads Are Probably Bleeding Budget

Most SC solar businesses default to Google Ads because the intent signal is obvious - someone searching "solar installation Sunshine Coast" is clearly in the market. But Google solar campaigns are among the most competitive and expensive in the home services category. Broad keyword targeting, low-quality score campaigns, and no landing page optimisation can push cost-per-lead well above $80-$120 per form submission. At those numbers, the economics only work if the quote-to-close rate is high.

Meta Ads solve a different problem for solar. The SC homeowner who has been vaguely aware that solar is a good idea but has not actively started shopping yet - that buyer is accessible on Facebook and Instagram. Scroll-stopping creative showing quarterly bill savings, battery payback periods, and completed installation photos reaches that buyer at the aspiration stage, before they type a search query. The cost-per-lead on well-structured Meta solar campaigns is typically lower, and the volume is higher.

The right channel mix for SC solar is not either/or. Google captures active searchers at the bottom of the funnel. Meta creates pipeline at the top. Running both with clear budget allocation between them, and tracking both through to booked quotes, is how SC solar businesses lower their blended CPL without reducing volume.

---

## The Follow-Up Gap: Where Solar Revenue Disappears

A homeowner in Mooloolaba fills in a solar quote request form on a Tuesday evening. You send a quote by Thursday. By the following Monday, they have three quotes in their inbox and you have heard nothing back.

This is the most common point where SC solar revenue disappears - not in the advertising, but in what happens after the form submission.

Research shows 78% of buyers hire the first business to respond (industry surveys). For solar, where the decision timeline is two to four weeks rather than two to four hours, that stat translates differently than it does for emergency trades. The buyer is not hiring the first responder on day one. They're responding to whichever business stays in front of them professionally across the decision window.

Research shows conversion risk increases 10x when you delay the first response by 5 minutes (Harvard Business Review). That first-touch speed matters. But so does the follow-up cadence: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 - each message sharing something useful, a bill savings calculation, a completed installation case study, a finance option, a time-limited offer. Research shows 60%+ of quotes convert after follow-up (Fergus 2024 AU trades data). Solar comparison shoppers who go quiet after week one are not always gone - they are often waiting for a business to demonstrate it genuinely wants their job.

## The System That Books More Sunshine Coast Solar Quotes

The mechanism is straightforward: fast first-touch response, automated follow-up sequence, and a client management system (CRM) that shows you exactly where every lead sits in the pipeline.

**Step 1: The form submission comes in.** Whether from a Meta lead form, a Google Ads landing page, or an organic enquiry, the lead enters the system immediately.

**Step 2: The AI sends an SMS within 5 minutes.** Not an auto-email that goes to spam. An SMS: "Hi [Name], thanks for your solar enquiry. We'll be in touch shortly to confirm your quote details - is there a good time to call?" First-touch response happens before any other business has picked up the phone.

**Step 3: The follow-up sequence runs automatically.** Day 3 shares a local case study or bill savings estimate. Day 7 follows up on quote status. Day 14 re-engages with a finance option or installation timeline. The sequence stops when the lead books or explicitly opts out.

**Step 4: The client management system (CRM) shows the full pipeline.** Every SC solar enquiry sits in a visible stage: new lead, contacted, quote sent, follow-up 1, follow-up 2, won, lost. You see the conversion rate at each stage and know exactly where leads are dropping.
