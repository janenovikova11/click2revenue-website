## Why Commercial Solar Marketing Requires a Different Approach

**The buyer is not one person.** A commercial solar project above $100K typically involves three to five decision-makers: the property owner or CEO who controls capital allocation, the CFO or finance manager who evaluates the payback period and financing options, and the facilities manager who manages the operational relationship with the installer and understands the roof structure and energy consumption patterns.

Messaging that speaks to the CEO doesn't convert the CFO. Copy built for the facilities manager doesn't reach the property owner. Effective commercial solar marketing delivers role-specific content to each stakeholder at the right point in the decision cycle.

**The timeline is measured in months, not days.** A commercial buyer who receives a proposal in January may not be ready to commit until their Q3 budget review. The marketing system has to maintain presence and provide value throughout that 6 to 18 month window — without burning the relationship by being too aggressive.

**The proof requirements are higher.** A Brisbane commercial property owner investing $500K in a rooftop PV system is not going to move based on a testimonial from a residential customer in Coorparoo. They want to see comparable commercial projects: system size, installation timeline, documented energy yield, payback period, and references from businesses similar to their own.
