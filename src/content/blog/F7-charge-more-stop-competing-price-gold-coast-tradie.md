# How to Charge More and Stop Competing on Price as a Gold Coast Tradie

Competing on price is a trap. Once you're in it, every job becomes a negotiation you're likely to lose — because there's always someone cheaper.

The Gold Coast has two very different types of trade customers. There's the customer who gets three quotes and picks the lowest number. And there's the customer with a $1.5 million home in Mermaid Beach who cares far more about reliability, speed, and professionalism than about saving $200. These two customers are not the same, and trying to serve both simultaneously is what keeps most Gold Coast tradies stuck.

The business problem is not your pricing. It's your positioning. You're attracting the wrong type of customer — and the systems you're running (or not running) are a large part of why.

---

## Price Competition Is a Positioning Problem

When a homeowner contacts three tradies and all three give a similar quote, they pick on price. That's rational behaviour.

But when a homeowner is referred to one tradie by a neighbour, or finds one tradie specifically recommended by ChatGPT or Google, or receives a quote within 20 minutes of calling while the other two tradies haven't called back yet — the comparison dynamic changes completely.

At that point, the conversation is not "who is cheapest?" It's "do I trust this business?" And trust-based decisions tolerate much higher price points.

The Gold Coast has a significant population of affluent property owners — particularly across Broadbeach, Mermaid Beach, Burleigh Heads, and Hope Island — who actively prefer to pay more for a tradie who responds fast, communicates clearly, and has strong reviews. They've had bad experiences with cheap tradies. They don't want to repeat them.

They just can't find the better alternative. That's the gap. Fill it, and price stops being the conversation.

---

## Why the Obvious Fix Fails

Most tradies who want to charge more try one of two things: they raise their prices and hope nobody notices, or they invest in a logo and a website refresh.

Neither works in isolation.

Raising prices without changing how your business is discovered and how it communicates signals nothing to the customer. The higher quote arrives looking exactly like all the other quotes — same format, same timing, same lack of follow-up. There's nothing in the experience to justify the premium.

A better logo doesn't change response times. It doesn't change the fact that your competitor called back in 15 minutes and you called back in three days. It doesn't change the fact that the customer checked ChatGPT and found a competitor mentioned by name, not you.

Positioning is not about aesthetics. It's about the experience the customer has from the first moment of contact.

---

## The Three Positioning Levers That Allow Higher Prices

### 1. Speed of Response

The fastest tradie to respond wins a disproportionate share of jobs — and wins them at higher prices, because the customer doesn't have time to shop around.

Industry surveys show that 78% of buyers hire the first business to respond. Research on lead conversion indicates that a 5-minute delay increases conversion risk tenfold; at 10 minutes, the risk is 100 times higher.

When your AI receptionist answers the call at 6:30pm and sends an SMS within five minutes — while your competitors' phones ring out — you're not competing anymore. You're selected.

The premium customer doesn't want the cheapest tradie. They want the one who makes them feel like a priority from the first call. Fast response does that better than any discount.

### 2. Professional Follow-Through

Tradies who send a quote and never follow up look the same as tradies who send a quote and do follow up — until three days later when the silence is noticed. At that point, the customer's confidence in the quality of the work they'd receive starts to erode.

Automated SMS follow-up sequences change this. After a quote is sent, the client receives a Day 1 message confirming you're available to answer questions. Day 3, a brief follow-up. Day 7 and Day 14, a final touchpoint.

This is not aggressive sales behaviour. It's the professional communication standard that premium clients expect. It signals that you run a tight operation — which justifies a premium rate.

Industry data indicates that 60% or more of quotes convert after follow-up. Most tradies never send one. Running a follow-up sequence is a competitive advantage by default.

### 3. AI Visibility (GEO/AEO)

According to Gartner, 94% of B2B buyers now use AI tools for supplier research. The same trend is reaching residential consumers. People are opening ChatGPT and Perplexity and asking: "Who is the best electrician in Burleigh Heads?" or "Which Gold Coast plumber has the best reviews?"

AI search optimisation (GEO/AEO) are the disciplines that get your business cited as the answer.

When ChatGPT names your business in response to that query, the customer who finds you is not the customer shopping on price. They're the customer who asked an AI to find them the best option. They've done one step of research, received a recommendation, and are now calling you.

That customer pays more. They complain less. They refer others.

Craig Davis, who runs Surf Hire Noosa with Click2Revenue's systems, achieved the number one position on ChatGPT for relevant searches in the Noosa area. The same AI search optimisation (GEO/AEO) strategy applies directly to Gold Coast trade businesses.

## What This Looks Like for a Gold Coast Tradie

A Gold Coast electrical contractor implements the three-lever system:

The AI receptionist answers every call, including evenings and weekends, with a professional voice response followed by an immediate SMS. Quote follow-up runs automatically across four touchpoints. The business's Google profile, website content, and online presence are optimised for AI citation.

Three months later, the type of lead changes. Fewer calls from people asking "what's your cheapest rate?" More calls from homeowners in Mermaid Beach and Hope Island who say "ChatGPT recommended you" or "my neighbour said you were the one to call."

These clients don't ask for three quotes. They already chose you before they called.

The price conversation becomes: "Here's our standard rate for this job." And the client says fine.

That's the shift. Not a better logo. Not a lower price. A better positioning system.
