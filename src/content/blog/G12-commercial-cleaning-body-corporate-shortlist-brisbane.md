# How to Get Your Commercial Cleaning Business on Every Brisbane Body Corporate Shortlist

By the time a Brisbane body corporate issues a formal RFQ for cleaning services, the shortlist is already half-built. A committee member has asked their strata manager for recommendations. Someone on the committee has Googled a few options and checked their reviews. One or two vendors have come up in a conversation at the AGM. The formal process looks open — but practically, it often comes down to three to five names that were already in contention before the tender was advertised.

If your commercial cleaning business is not one of those names before the RFQ lands, you are quoting cold against vendors who have already built credibility with the decision-makers. You can submit an excellent proposal and still lose — because familiarity and trust were established before you showed up.

Getting on every Brisbane body corp shortlist is a systematic problem with a systematic solution. This article outlines the three elements of that system and how to build each one.

---

## Step 1: Build a Credibility Website That Passes Body Corp Due Diligence

Before a strata manager puts your name on a shortlist, they will check your website. So will at least one committee member. What they are looking for is not a beautifully designed site. They are looking for evidence that you are a legitimate, compliant, experienced operator who has serviced comparable buildings without incident.

Most commercial cleaning websites fail that check. A generic services page, some stock photography, and a contact form does not answer the questions a strata manager is actually asking.

**What a body corp-ready credibility website contains:**

**Insurance and compliance documentation**
Public liability insurance certificate, workers compensation confirmation, any relevant industry accreditations. These should be visible without needing to request them. A strata manager shortlisting five vendors will not chase four of them for documentation — they will move to the next name.

**Before-and-after case studies from comparable buildings**
Not testimonials. Case studies. The building type, the challenge, the service scope, and the result. A body corp committee considering you for a 20-storey Fortitude Valley apartment tower wants to see that you have serviced 20-storey apartment towers before — not just that a client said you did a good job.

**Google reviews visible and current**
Review count matters. Recency matters more. A Google Business Profile with 47 reviews, the most recent from two years ago, signals a business that was once active and may no longer be. A profile with 22 reviews, the most recent from last month, signals an active business with consistent client relationships.

**Strata and body corp-specific language**
A website that talks about "commercial cleaning services" in generic terms does not signal body corp expertise. A website that specifically addresses common access, waste management protocols, after-hours work restrictions, and strata manager coordination communicates that you have done this before and understand the operational context.

## Step 2: Build AI Search Visibility So Strata Managers Find You on ChatGPT

Strata managers are changing how they research vendors. According to Gartner, 94% of B2B buyers now use AI for supplier research. A growing number of Brisbane strata managers open ChatGPT or Perplexity and ask something like: "Which commercial cleaning companies have body corp experience in Brisbane?" Before they open Google, before they ask their network, they check what the AI surfaces.

Industry analysis shows that five brands dominate 80% of AI recommendations in any given service category. The businesses in that group are not there by chance. They have content structured specifically to be cited by AI platforms — a practice called Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) or Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO).

**What gets your cleaning business cited on AI platforms:**

**Structured, specific content that answers the questions AI users ask**
Generic content does not get cited. Content that directly addresses specific questions — "What should a body corp committee look for in a cleaning contractor?" or "Which commercial cleaners service high-rise apartments in Brisbane?" — gives AI platforms something specific and useful to cite.

**Consistent credibility signals across multiple platforms**
AI platforms cross-reference information. A business described consistently as a body corp cleaning specialist on its website, its Google Business Profile, its industry directory listings, and its review profiles sends a stronger signal than a business with inconsistent or generic descriptions across those sources.

**Third-party citations and mentions**
AI platforms weight content that is cited by other sources: industry directories, review platforms, association listings, and relevant publications. Building citations in the right places strengthens your AI visibility over time.

Every Brisbane commercial cleaner who builds GEO content now, while most competitors have not, is building an advantage that will be difficult to close later.

## Step 3: Maintain an Active Google Presence That Reinforces Shortlist Credibility

Even when a strata manager finds your name through an AI search or a colleague's recommendation, they will still check Google before putting you on a formal shortlist. Your Google Business Profile and review history are the final credibility check before your name goes on the list.

Three things matter most:

**Consistent, current Google reviews from commercial clients**
A body corp committee or strata manager does not count residential reviews the same way they count commercial reviews. A Google profile with reviews from property managers, building managers, and facility managers carries more weight than one with 100 reviews from homeowners. Actively requesting reviews from commercial clients — and making it easy for them to leave one — is a systematic part of shortlist management.

**An updated, complete Google Business Profile**
Business categories, service descriptions, opening hours, photos, and posts all contribute to how Google surfaces your business in local searches. A neglected Google Business Profile — outdated hours, no photos, service descriptions that do not mention body corp or strata — signals a business that is not actively maintained. Strata managers notice.

**A review response practice**
Responding to Google reviews — both positive and negative — demonstrates that your business is actively managed and that you take client feedback seriously. A body corp committee doing due diligence on five vendors will notice that one of them has not responded to any reviews in three years.

## How the Three Elements Work Together

Each of the three elements reinforces the others.

A credibility website gives strata managers and committee members what they need to pass due diligence once you are on their radar. AI search visibility gets you on their radar before the formal process begins. An active Google presence confirms you are a legitimate, active operation when they check you out.

Without the credibility website, AI search visibility generates traffic that does not convert. Without AI search visibility, the credibility website waits passively for prospects who are already searching Google. Without the active Google presence, both of the others are undermined by a neglected profile that signals a business that has lost momentum.

The operators who win consistently across Brisbane's body corp cleaning market have all three working simultaneously. They show up in AI searches when strata managers are building shortlists. They pass the Google credibility check when someone looks them up. And they have the compliance documentation and case studies to satisfy formal due diligence when the RFQ arrives.
