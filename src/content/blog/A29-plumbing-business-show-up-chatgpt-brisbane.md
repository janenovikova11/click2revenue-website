## Expert Insight: What GEO and AEO Actually Mean

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation. AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimisation. Both refer to the same shift: creating content designed to be cited by AI models, not just ranked by traditional search algorithms.

The mechanism is different from SEO in several important ways.

Traditional SEO rewards page authority, backlink volume, and keyword density. AI search optimisation (GEO/AEO) rewards specificity, local context, and answer quality. A 2,000-word article that directly answers the question "what should I do if my hot water system fails in Brisbane in winter?" will consistently outperform a generic "plumbing services Brisbane" landing page in AI results.

AI models are also sensitive to trust signals within the content itself. Mentioning real locations - Coorparoo, Ashgrove, Chermside, the inner west - signals local relevance. Using named examples, quoting realistic scenarios, and writing with a clear point of view all increase the likelihood of citation. Generic, filler-heavy content gets ignored.

This is not a trick or a shortcut. It is a discipline that requires consistent, well-researched, locally specific content production. Most plumbing businesses do not have the time or the brief to do it well.
