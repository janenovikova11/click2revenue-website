# How to Qualify Trade Leads So You Only Quote Jobs Worth Winning on the Sunshine Coast

**Not every lead is worth chasing. The tradies growing fastest on the Sunshine Coast have stopped quoting everything and started quoting the right things.**

---

## The Problem With Saying Yes to Every Enquiry

Running a trades business on the Sunshine Coast sounds like it should be straightforward right now. The Sunshine Coast housing market is one of the strongest in Queensland. New estates are opening from Caloundra South through to Peregian Springs. Renovation activity across Noosa, Buderim, and the Hinterland is consistent. The demand is real.

The problem is not a lack of enquiries. For most SC tradie businesses running at capacity, the problem is the wrong enquiries eating up quoting time that could go to the right ones.

A typical week might look like this: three quotes go to jobs outside your profitable service area. Two quotes go to homeowners who are price-shopping across five contractors and will take the lowest number regardless of quality. One job turns out to be a small repair that does not justify the drive or the admin. By the end of the week, your quoting hours are gone and the jobs you actually wanted are already booked with someone else.

This is a qualification problem, not a marketing problem. And it does not get fixed by generating more leads.

---

## Why Quoting Everything Is Costing You

Most tradie businesses on the Sunshine Coast use no formal qualification process. A call comes in, someone picks up, and if it sounds like a job, a quote goes out. The filter is usually gut feel or the owner's bandwidth that day.

The consequence shows up in the numbers. Consider the time cost of a quote that does not convert: drive time to and from the site, the assessment, writing the quote, following up. For a typical trades job, that is two to four hours of productive time gone. If four in ten of those quotes go to jobs that were never realistic wins, that is a significant loss of capacity every single month.

And because the business is busy enough to keep going, the pattern stays invisible. The owner knows something is not quite right with the margin or the conversion rate, but the root cause is buried in the quoting process rather than anywhere obvious.

The fix is to put a filter in front of your phone before unqualified leads reach you.

---

## Three Things That Qualify a Lead Before It Hits Your Diary

A well-configured AI pre-qualification system screens every inbound enquiry against three criteria before anything goes to a human.

**1. Location**

Every trades business has a profitable service zone. On the Sunshine Coast, that might be Noosa to Caloundra, or it might be tighter than that. Leads from outside that zone cost more to service and often produce lower margins. An AI intake system captures the property postcode or suburb at the start of every enquiry and flags or redirects anything outside your zone automatically. Wrong-postcode jobs stop reaching your quoting calendar.

**2. Job type and value threshold**

Not every job type suits your business model. If your crew is structured for bathroom renovations and multi-day electrical upgrades, small repair calls are a mismatch regardless of the customer's budget. AI pre-qualification captures job type and estimated scope in the first exchange and scores enquiries based on whether they match what your business actually wants to be doing. Low-value or mismatched job types get handled differently from the start, whether that means referral, a minimum call-out fee upfront, or a polite decline.

**3. Budget and timeline signals**

Enquiries from homeowners who want three quotes for a $500 repair or who are not planning to proceed for six months occupy the same quoting slot as a serious $15,000 renovation job. A qualification sequence that asks the right questions early, using AI SMS within the first five minutes of enquiry, surfaces these signals before any time is invested. Leads with buying intent get fast-tracked. Leads without it go into a low-cost nurture sequence that keeps the door open without consuming your capacity.
