# How to Follow Up on Trade Quotes Automatically Without Hiring Admin

You send the quote. Then you wait. A few days pass and you think about following up, but you're flat out on-site and it slips. The homeowner gets a second quote from someone else. They go with them. You never found out why.

This is not a sales problem. It is a process problem. And hiring another admin does not fix it.

---

## The Problem: Cold Quotes Cost You More Than You Think

Most Brisbane trades businesses send quotes and rely on memory, a spreadsheet, or a good intention to follow up. None of those scale.

The numbers are confronting. More than 60% of quotes convert after a structured follow-up sequence, according to Fergus (2024). That means the majority of your cold pipeline is not dead - it is just sitting there, waiting for someone to push.

The average job value in most trades is not trivial. A missed electrical quote might be $800. A bathroom renovation quote is $12,000. A missed plumbing inspection is $1,500. Multiply that across a pipeline of 20 or 30 cold quotes and you are looking at serious revenue sitting idle.

Hiring admin to chase quotes sounds logical until you work out the maths. A full-time admin costs $55,000-$70,000 per year. A part-timer still costs $30,000-plus. And humans forget, get sick, and go on leave. The quotes still go cold.

---

## Expert Insight: Why Manual Follow-Up Fails Trades Businesses

The core issue is timing. Research from Harvard Business Review shows that a 5-minute delay in responding to a warm lead reduces conversion by 10 times. At 10 minutes, the drop is 100 times.

Most tradies are on the tools. You cannot respond to a quote request in 5 minutes while you are under a house or up a ladder. And once a quote has gone out, chasing it at day 8 by phone - when you finally have a spare moment - is almost always too late.

The homeowner has either moved on or forgotten why they chose you. They are comparing on price now, not on trust. You have lost the relationship advantage.

What works is a structured, automated follow-up sequence that runs without you. Day 1, day 3, day 7, day 14. Each message is different in tone and approach. Early messages are soft and helpful. Later messages create urgency or offer a simple reason to move forward. The sequence runs whether you are on-site or on holidays.

This is not about being pushy. It is about staying in the conversation long enough for the homeowner to make a decision.

**Day 3 - Check-in.** A short follow-up SMS or email asks if they have any questions. Most people have not made a decision yet - they are busy. This keeps you front of mind without being annoying.

**Day 7 - Gentle urgency.** The message references availability. Something like: "We have a slot opening up next week if you want to lock in a start date." This gives the homeowner a practical reason to reply.

**Day 14 - Last touch.** The final message is brief and low-pressure. It gives the homeowner an easy out while leaving the door open. Something like: "Still happy to hold your spot - just let us know either way."

At each stage, if the homeowner replies, the sequence pauses and the job moves into your active pipeline. You only get involved when someone says yes or asks a question.
