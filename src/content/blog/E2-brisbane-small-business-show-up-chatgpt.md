## Why "Just Do More SEO" Does Not Fix This

The instinct most Brisbane business owners have is to lean harder on what already works: more Google Ads, better website SEO, more reviews on Google. Those tactics still matter. But they do not solve the AI visibility problem, because AI engines do not work like Google search.

Google ranks pages. AI engines synthesise answers. When someone asks ChatGPT for a recommendation, the AI is not returning a list of ranked URLs. It is constructing an answer from content it has processed and determined to be authoritative, specific, and citation-worthy.

The businesses AI engines cite are the ones whose content clearly and directly answers the question being asked. Generic service pages, vague "about us" copy, and keyword-stuffed blog posts do not get cited. Structured, specific, authoritative content does.

More ad spend will not get you cited on ChatGPT. Better Google rankings help but do not guarantee AI citation. You need a different strategy.

## The Mechanism: GEO and AEO

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. Both refer to the practice of structuring your content so that AI engines are more likely to pull from it when constructing answers.

The core principles:

**Answer specific questions directly.** AI engines are looking for content that answers real questions with real specificity. "What is the best way to clean a strata building lobby?" answered with specific methods, frequencies, and product choices is citation-worthy. A page that says "we offer premium commercial cleaning services" is not.

**Build authoritative signals.** AI engines build entity associations. They connect your business name to your category, your location, and your credibility signals. Consistent, accurate business information across directories, review platforms, and industry publications all feed this.

**Use structured formats.** Headers, lists, and clear question-and-answer structures make it easier for AI engines to extract and cite specific information. Walls of flowing prose are harder to parse.

**Publish regularly.** AI engines favour businesses with a sustained pattern of authoritative content publication. A one-off article does not build citation presence. A consistent cadence does.

**Target the questions your customers actually ask AI.** This is the research layer. We identify what your ideal customer is typing into ChatGPT and Perplexity, and we build content that directly answers those questions in a way AI engines can cite.

None of this is traditional SEO. It borrows some of the same principles — authority, specificity, consistency — but the execution is different. A content strategy optimised for AI citation looks different from one optimised for Google keyword rankings.
