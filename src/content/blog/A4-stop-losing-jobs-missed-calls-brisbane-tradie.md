# How to Stop Losing Jobs When You Miss Calls as a Brisbane Tradie

You're under a sink in Paddington. Your phone rings. You can't answer. You finish the job 40 minutes later, see the missed call, ring back. No answer. They've already booked someone else.

This happens to most Brisbane tradies multiple times a week. It doesn't feel like a crisis in the moment — you're busy, you'll call back, you've got work. But industry data puts a number on it. An estimated $52,000 per year is the average revenue lost from just 2-4 missed calls per week. Not because the leads were bad. Because you didn't answer first.

---

## The Real Cost of a Missed Call

When a Brisbane homeowner has a plumbing emergency, a failed aircon in summer, or a renovation quote they need, they don't leave one voicemail and wait patiently. They call three to five tradies and book whoever answers first.

Research shows 78% of buyers hire the first business to respond (industry surveys). That's not the best-priced option. Not the most experienced tradie. The first one to respond.

The math from there is straightforward. If an average job is worth $600-$1,500 and you're missing 2-4 calls a week across the year, an estimated $52,000 in annual revenue walks out the door. Some missed calls are duds. Many aren't. The problem is you never find out which was which.

There's a delay factor on top of that. Industry data shows conversion risk increases 10x when you delay a callback by 5 minutes (industry data). At 10 minutes, conversion risk increases 100x. Even a fast callback 30-40 minutes later is statistically unlikely to win the job.

---

## Why the Obvious Fix Doesn't Work

The first solution most tradies try is a VA or a receptionist. Hire someone to answer the phone so you don't have to.

This works until it doesn't. A VA costs $800-$2,000 a month and operates business hours only. The majority of urgent calls — leaks, electrical faults, emergency HVAC — come after hours, on weekends, or during the exact windows when a VA has knocked off. The phone still goes unanswered. The job still goes to a competitor.

Voicemail doesn't solve it either. A voicemail message is not a response. The buyer has already moved on to the next number before they finish leaving a message.

The real constraint is time. You're on the tools. You can't answer every call. And you can't hire a person who's available at 11pm on a Sunday for the same price as a software subscription.

---

## The Mechanism: AI Answers, Qualifies, and Follows Up

The fix is an AI receptionist that operates 24/7 and responds within 5 minutes of every missed call.

Here is what the system does:

**Step 1: The call comes in.** You're on a job in Chermside. The phone rings and you can't answer.

**Step 2: The AI picks up or the system captures the missed call.** The caller either speaks to the AI voice agent or receives an SMS within 5 minutes: "Hi, this is [Your Business], sorry we missed you — what can we help with today?"

**Step 3: The lead is qualified.** The AI gathers the job type, location, urgency, and preferred callback time. That information goes straight into the client management system (CRM). You see it when you come off the job.

**Step 4: The follow-up sequence starts.** If the lead doesn't book immediately, they enter an automated SMS sequence: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14. Industry data shows 60%+ of quotes convert after follow-up — jobs that would otherwise have gone cold.

The buyer gets an immediate response. You get a qualified lead in your client management system (CRM). No VA. No missed opportunities at 10pm.
