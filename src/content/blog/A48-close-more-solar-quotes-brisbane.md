# How to Close More Solar Quotes in Brisbane Before Customers Get Three Other Quotes

Every Brisbane solar installer knows the pattern. A homeowner fills out an enquiry form. They also fill out two or three more — on comparison sites, on competitor websites, directly from a Google Ad they clicked 30 seconds earlier. Within 10 minutes, they've submitted to four businesses. Within 2 weeks, they've signed with one. The other three get a polite "we went another way."

The deciding factor is almost never price. It's speed and presence. The solar installer who responds first shapes the buyer's reference point. The one who stays in contact throughout the 1-2 week decision window stays in the conversation. The ones who send a quote and wait get a two-line rejection email.

The mechanism that determines which Brisbane solar business closes first is not complicated. But most solar businesses don't have it built.

---

## The Problem: Solar Buyers Decide Fast and You Don't Know When

The Clean Energy Regulator data shows SE QLD has some of the highest residential solar adoption rates in Australia. Brisbane's market is competitive and reasonably mature. That means most Brisbane homeowners who enquire about solar have done some research before they fill in the first form. They know roughly what system size they want, what a reasonable price looks like, and how the rebate structure works.

That prior research compresses the decision timeline. A buyer who has already spent 3 hours on solar comparison sites before enquiring doesn't need 6 weeks to decide. They need 1 to 2 weeks to compare quotes, check the installers' Google reviews, ask a question or two, and sign.

Industry data shows solar buyers typically get 3 or more quotes and decide within 1 to 2 weeks (Clean Energy Regulator 2024). In that window, most of the decision is made in the first 48 hours. The installer who has the first substantive conversation with the buyer holds a significant advantage.

The problem is that most Brisbane solar businesses don't know exactly when a buyer is in their decision window. The homeowner doesn't call to say "I'm comparing you against two others right now." They just go quiet after the quote. And then they sign elsewhere.

---

## Why Slow Response and No Follow-Up Are the Real Conversion Killers

Research shows that conversion risk rises 10x when a lead waits more than 5 minutes for a response (Harvard Business Review). In solar, where a homeowner submits to multiple businesses simultaneously, a 5-minute gap is the difference between being first in the conversation and being third.

A Brisbane solar business with a standard office phone setup and business hours coverage cannot respond to a 7pm enquiry at 7:02pm. That lead waits until the next morning. By 8am, two or three other installers have already sent a response. Some may have already booked a site visit.

The second failure point is post-quote follow-up. A homeowner who receives a quote and doesn't respond within 2 days is not necessarily gone. They're busy. They received 3 quotes on the same day and haven't had time to sit down and compare them. They had a question about panel placement but didn't want to call. They're waiting for a weekend to talk it over with their partner.

The installer who follows up on Day 1, Day 3, and Day 7 is present during that decision process. The one who sends the quote and waits has effectively removed themselves from the conversation.

Industry data shows 60%+ of quotes convert after follow-up (Fergus 2024). That is a substantial proportion of the revenue sitting in every solar business's sent quotes folder, attached to leads that just needed one more touchpoint.

---

## The Mechanism: 5-Minute Response, Instant Booking, Proof-Led Nurture

Three specific tools change the close rate for Brisbane solar businesses.

**Tool 1: AI SMS within 5 minutes of every enquiry**

Every inbound enquiry — form submission, missed call, after-hours contact — triggers an AI SMS within 5 minutes. The message is personalised: it references what the homeowner enquired about, confirms you're taking new Brisbane solar installs, and provides a direct booking link for a site assessment.

This positions your business as the first to respond, every time, regardless of when the enquiry comes in. A 9pm enquiry gets a 9:02pm response. An early morning Saturday form fill gets an immediate acknowledgement. The buyer knows someone is on their enquiry before they've even opened the next installer's website.

**Tool 2: Instant booking link**

The SMS includes a link to book a site assessment directly into your calendar. No phone tag. No waiting for someone to check availability and call back tomorrow. The homeowner can lock in a time within 2 minutes of receiving the SMS.

Research shows 78% of buyers hire the first business to respond (Harvard Business Review). Being first to provide a booking link is being first to secure the site assessment. And the solar installer who does the site assessment first almost always provides the reference point that every subsequent quote is compared against.

**Tool 3: Proof-led nurture sequence through the 14-day decision window**

After the initial response, our platform runs a structured nurture sequence through the buyer's 1 to 2 week decision cycle.

Day 1: Initial AI SMS response with booking link.
Day 3: Follow-up SMS with a Brisbane solar case study and Google review link — social proof while the homeowner is still comparing.
Day 7: Personalised follow-up referencing their system size and any specific questions from the first conversation.
Day 14: Final check-in with a clear prompt to confirm their installation slot before the current schedule fills.

Each touchpoint keeps your business in the conversation without being pushy. The homeowner is comparing three quotes. You're the only one staying in contact. That presence matters at decision time.
