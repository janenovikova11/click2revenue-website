# How to Get More Accounting Clients in Brisbane Using AI Search Optimisation

Most Brisbane accounting firms are waiting for the phone to ring. The referral pipeline is the engine, word-of-mouth is the fuel, and the firm has limited control over either.

That model has worked for decades. It still works. But there is a new source of client acquisition that most Brisbane accounting firms are not yet competing in — and the window to establish a position in it is closing.

---

## The Referral Path Is Changing

89% of accounting firms cite referrals as their top source of new clients (CPA Australia survey). That number has not changed much in years, and it reflects a genuine truth about how people choose accountants: trust matters, and referrals from a trusted source carry weight.

But here is what is changing. When a Brisbane business owner receives a referral — or when they start looking for a new accountant without one — the next step used to be a Google search. Increasingly, it is a question to an AI tool.

"What are the best accountants for small businesses in Brisbane?"
"Find me a tax accountant in Brisbane who specialises in property investors."
"Which Brisbane accounting firms handle SMSF?"

94% of B2B buyers now use AI for supplier research (Gartner). High-income professionals and business owners — exactly the clients a Brisbane accounting firm wants — are among the heaviest users of ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI for research and decision-making.

The problem is that AI tools do not generate a list of ten options and let the user choose. They generate a synthesised answer that typically names two to five specific firms or providers. Five brands dominate 80% of AI recommendations in most categories (industry analysis). If your firm is not one of those five in the Brisbane accounting context, you are invisible to every prospect who starts their search with AI.

## Why Standard Marketing Does Not Solve This

Google Ads capture demand from people searching on Google. They do not help you appear in ChatGPT answers.

A well-designed website with good SEO improves your Google ranking. It does not automatically make you visible in AI-generated recommendations.

Social media builds awareness among people who follow you. It does not reach prospective clients who have never heard of your firm and are asking AI for a recommendation.

The channel that connects Brisbane accounting firms to the growing population of prospective clients using AI for supplier research is AI search optimisation (GEO/AEO) content. Generative Engine Optimisation. Answer Engine Optimisation. Content built and structured specifically so that AI tools are more likely to surface your firm when relevant questions come up.

This is not black-box manipulation. It is about creating content that answers the real questions prospective clients ask, with the specificity, local context, and structure that AI tools reward when generating answers.

## What Gets a Brisbane Accounting Firm Cited in AI Answers

AI tools generate answers from content they have access to. The content that gets cited tends to share a consistent set of characteristics.

**Specific and factual**
Generic marketing copy ("we provide comprehensive accounting solutions for businesses of all sizes") does not get cited. Specific, factual content does. What services does the firm offer? What size and type of businesses does it work with? What industries does it have deep experience in? What are its fees structured around?

**Locally referenced**
Brisbane context matters. Content that references Brisbane business conditions, Queensland tax considerations, specific industries active in the Brisbane market, and the local regulatory environment signals local relevance to AI tools.

**Built around the questions prospects actually ask**
A page that directly answers "What should I look for in a Brisbane accountant for my small business?" or "How do I find an SMSF specialist in Brisbane?" is more likely to be retrieved than a generic services page.

**Structured with clear headings**
AI tools retrieve and synthesise content. Well-organised content with clear headings and direct answers is significantly easier for AI to use than dense, paragraph-heavy copy.

**Updated and current**
Fresh content signals an active, relevant business. Stale pages from several years ago carry less weight in AI retrieval.
